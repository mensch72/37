How to play

37 is a three-player game on a hexagon of 37 cells. Each bird (Red, Yellow, Blue) owns one pair of opposite sides of the boulder. You win by joining your two sides with an unbroken chain of your own lichen — the shortest possible chain is 7 cells, hence the name.

Your move: either drop a fleck of lichen from your beak onto an empty cell, or pick one of your own cells back into your beak. Then the whole boulder grows at once:

  • a living cell with 0 neighbours dies of isolation;
  • a living cell with 1–4 neighbours survives;
  • a living cell with 5–6 neighbours dies of crowding;
  • an empty cell with exactly 4 neighbours is born — its colour is the majority of its four parents, the plurality in a 2-1-1, or, in a 2-2 tie, the absent third colour.

Newborn flecks come from the box and dead flecks return to it; your beak only ever exchanges flecks with the boulder. Your beak starts with N flecks — the whole difficulty dial. A move may not recreate a position that has already occurred; a player with no cells and an empty beak is out.

Colours are shown by hue and shape (Red ▲, Yellow ●, Blue ■) so the board stays readable without relying on the red–green axis.

A static web app — everything, including the learned computer player, runs in your browser. Rules and design from the sandbox/ exploration in this repository.
